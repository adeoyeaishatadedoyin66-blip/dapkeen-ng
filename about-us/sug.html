• SUPPLY OF GRAINS

A grain is a small, hard, dry edible fruit or seed harvested for human or animal consumption. They are a fundamental part of the global diet, providing essential carbohydrates, protein, and vitamins.

Key Points:

Types: Grains are broadly divided into two categories:

Cereal grains (true grains) are members of the grass family and include wheat, rice, corn (maize), barley, oats, and rye. Pseudocereals are non-grass plants that produce grain-like seeds, such as quinoa, buckwheat, and amaranth.

Value: Grains are a primary source of energy-giving carbohydrates. Whole grains (containing the bran, germ, and endosperm) are particularly nutritious, offering significant amounts of fiber, B vitamins, and minerals. Refined grains have the bran and germ removed, which reduces their nutritional content.

Storage and Trade: Due to their small, hard, and dry nature, grains are durable and can be stored and transported easily, making them a major global agricultural commodity.

Uses: Grains are versatile and used in numerous ways:

Food: They are cooked whole, or ground into flour for making breads, pasta, and cakes.

Animal Feed: Large quantities of grains, particularly corn, are used as feed for livestock and poultry.

Industrial Products: Grains are also used to produce various industrial substances, including alcohols and vegetable oils
