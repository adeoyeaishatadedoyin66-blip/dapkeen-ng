  • SUPPLY OF GRANITE

Granite is a hard, coarse-grained, light-colored intrusive igneous rock primarily composed of quartz and feldspar, often with mica and other minerals. It forms deep within the Earth from slowly cooling magma and is incredibly durable, resistant to heat and abrasion, and widely used for countertops, monuments, and flooring. Its long-lasting nature makes it a common component of continental crust and mountain roots. and formation

Composition: Granite is made mostly of quartz and feldspar (both alkali and plagioclase), along with smaller amounts of other minerals like mica (biotite and muscovite), hornblende, and pyroxene.

Formation: It forms from the slow cooling and solidification of magma deep underground, which allows for the large crystal growth that gives granite its coarse-grained texture.

Location: It is the most common plutonic rock in the Earth's continental crust and often forms the cores of continents and the roots of mountain ranges.

Properties and uses

Properties: Granite is known for its high durability, strength, and resistance to heat, stains, and scratches. It also has low water absorption and can be polished to a high sheen.

Uses: Due to its properties, granite is a popular material for countertops, flooring, and building stone. It is also used in construction for structural components, decorative monuments, and as crushed stone for roads.
