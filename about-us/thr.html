A truck is a motor vehicle designed primarily for transporting goods, performing special tasks, or other utilitarian work. They vary greatly in size and configuration, from smaller vehicles similar to cars to very large, powerful commercial trucks. Many are built with body-on-frame construction, and common types include straight trucks with a single frame and articulated vehicles that are linked in sections.

Function: Trucks transport cargo and materials, or serve specialized roles like fire fighting or refuse collection.

Construction: Most trucks have a distinct cab for the driver, separate from the cargo area, and use body-on-frame construction. They are powered by an engine, with diesel being common, though electric models are increasing.

Types: Trucks can be classified as either straight trucks, where all axles are on a single frame, or articulated vehicles, which are connected by couplings and often include a trailer.

Key components: A truck's basic parts include the engine, chassis (including drivetrain, steering, and brakes), body, and electrical system.
